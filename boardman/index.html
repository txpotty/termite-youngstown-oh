Empowering Your Defenses: Detailed Termite Control Services

Our termite control services are comprehensive, addressing every aspect of a termite infestation from identification to prevention. We use a multi-step process to ensure effective and long-lasting results.

Thorough Termite Inspection

The first and most crucial step in effective termite control is a detailed inspection. Our trained technicians perform a meticulous examination of your property to identify signs of termite activity, locate potential entry points, and determine the extent of the infestation. We inspect both the interior and exterior of your home, including basements, crawl spaces, attics, and even landscaping.

Identifying Termite Species

Different types of termites require different treatment approaches. During the inspection, our experts are trained to identify the specific species of termites infesting your property, whether they are subterranean termites, drywood termites, or dampwood termites. This identification is vital for developing a targeted and effective treatment plan.

Advanced Inspection Techniques

We utilize advanced tools and techniques during our inspections, including moisture meters and infrared cameras, to detect hidden termite activity and assess moisture levels that may attract termites. This allows us to uncover even the most elusive colonies.

Customized Treatment Plans

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan tailored to the specific needs of your property and the type of termites present. We will discuss our findings with you and explain the proposed treatment options, ensuring you understand the process and feel comfortable with our recommendations.

Liquid Termiticide Barriers

One of the most effective methods for controlling subterranean termites is the strategic application of liquid termiticides around the perimeter of your home. This creates an undetectable barrier that eliminates termites as they attempt to reach your structure or return to their colony. We use only proven and effective termiticide products.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems are another highly effective tool, particularly for established colonies. These systems involve placing bait stations in the ground around your property. Termites discover the bait, consume it, and carry it back to the colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony. This can be a less disruptive method than liquid treatments in some situations.

Wood Treatment

For certain termite species or localized infestations, we may recommend direct wood treatment. This involves applying termiticides directly to infested wood or wood susceptible to infestation. This helps to eliminate existing termites within the wood and prevent future attacks.

Ongoing Monitoring and Prevention

Our commitment to protecting your home doesn't stop after the initial treatment. We offer ongoing monitoring services to ensure that termites don't return. This can involve periodic inspections and maintenance of baiting systems or liquid barriers. Prevention is key to long-term termite control.

Identifying and Addressing Risk Factors

During our services, we also identify and advise you on potential risk factors that could attract termites, such as leaky pipes, excessive moisture in crawl spaces, or wood-to-soil contact. Addressing these issues can significantly reduce the likelihood of future infestations.

Educating Homeowners

We believe in empowering our customers with knowledge. Our technicians will provide you with valuable information on how to maintain a termite-resistant environment around your home, including tips on proper drainage, landscaping, and wood storage. Understanding common termite behaviors can help you be proactive in protecting your property.

Empowering Your Defenses: Common Termite Problems and Solutions

Many homeowners are unaware of the subtle signs of a termite infestation until significant damage has occurred. Recognizing the early warning signs is crucial for prompt action and minimizing repair costs. Here are some common problems that indicate you may need our termite control services in Boardman, OH:

Finding Mud Tubes on Walls or Foundations

Subterranean termites build mud tubes, which are tunnel-like structures made from soil, wood, and their own saliva. These tubes serve as protected pathways for termites to travel between their underground colony and their food source (your home). If you see pencil-width mud tubes on your foundation, walls, or even in your basement or crawl space, it's a strong indicator of subterranean termite activity. These tubes protect the termites from predators and dehydration as they move. Our expert inspection will confirm if these tubes are active and lead to live colonies. We will then implement targeted treatments to eliminate the colony and prevent future tube formation.

Hearing Faint Tapping or Clicking Sounds

Soldier termites sometimes bang their heads against the wood or shake their bodies when the colony is disturbed. This creates a faint tapping or clicking sound that you might hear if you are close to an infested area. While often subtle, unusual sounds coming from your walls or wooden structures should not be ignored. These sounds are a form of communication within the termite colony, often signaling a threat or disturbance. Our advanced listening equipment can sometimes help pinpoint the location of termite activity based on these sounds, allowing for more precise and effective treatment.

Discovering Piles of Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites produce small, pellet-like droppings called frass. These droppings are often the same color as the wood the termites are consuming and may be found in small piles near infested wooden structures. The presence of frass is a clear indication of a drywood termite infestation. Unlike subterranean termites that live underground, drywood termites live entirely within the wood they infest, pushing their waste out through small kick-out holes. Finding these tiny piles of sawdust-like material near baseboards, doorframes, or furniture is a strong sign you need professional help. We will locate the infested wood and apply appropriate treatments to eliminate the colony within the structure.

Experiencing Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Significant termite damage can weaken the structural integrity of your home, leading to sagging floors, ceilings, or even walls. This is a sign of a severe and long-standing infestation where termites have consumed a substantial amount of wood. Sagging structures indicate that the weight of your home is no longer adequately supported due to damaged wooden beams or joists. This level of damage requires immediate attention to prevent further deterioration and potential safety hazards. Our team can assess the extent of the structural damage and recommend the necessary repairs in addition to providing comprehensive termite treatment to eliminate the source of the problem.

Noticing Damaged or Discolored Drywall or Plaster

Termites don't just eat wood; they can also damage drywall and plaster. As they tunnel behind these materials, they can cause them to become discolored, warped, or even develop small holes. The moisture associated with termite activity can also stain drywall. Changes in the appearance of your walls or ceilings without an obvious cause, such as water damage, should raise suspicion. Termites can create pathways behind drywall, feeding on the paper backing. Any unusual bubbling, cracking, or discoloration of your walls or ceilings should be investigated further. We can inspect affected areas to determine if termites are the culprit and implement targeted treatments.

Wood that Sounds Hollow When Tapped

When termites consume the interior of a wooden structure, they leave a hollow shell. Tapping on infested wood with a screwdriver or hammer will produce a hollow sound, as if the wood is empty inside. This is a simple but effective way to identify potential termite damage. As termites consume the cellulose within the wood, they hollow out galleries and tunnels, leaving only thin veneers. Tapping on the surface reveals the lack of solid material underneath. Any wood that sounds significantly different from solid wood when tapped should be an area of concern and warrants a professional inspection.

Finding Piles of Discarded Wings

Termites swarm at certain times of the year to reproduce and establish new colonies. During a swarm, winged reproductive termites leave their existing colony and fly in search of a mate and a suitable location to start a new nest. After swarming, these termites shed their wings, which can be found in piles near windowsills, doorways, or light sources. The presence of discarded wings is a tell-tale sign that a termite colony is nearby, even if you don't see the termites themselves. Swarming is often the first visible indication of a termite infestation, and finding these wings means it's time for a professional inspection to locate the active colony.

Stiff or Difficult to Open Windows or Doors

As termites consume wooden door and window frames, they can cause them to warp and become difficult to open or close. The damage caused by termites can distort the shape of frames, making them bind or stick. This issue is often mistaken for normal settling or humidity problems, but in conjunction with other signs, it could indicate termite activity. Termite damage compromises the structural integrity of the wood, leading to misalignment of windows and doors. If you notice your windows or doors are suddenly difficult to operate without an apparent reason, consider the possibility of termite infestation.

Pinpoint Holes in Drywall or Wood

These small holes, roughly the size of a pinhead, are sometimes created by termites as they push frass (droppings) or debris out of their tunnels. While not always present, these tiny holes can be another indicator of termite activity within walls or wooden structures. These 'kick-out' holes are common with drywood termites as they expel their waste from their galleries. Finding these small, often inconspicuous holes can be a subtle sign that warrants closer inspection by a professional to determine the extent of the infestation and the specific type of termite involved.

Presence of Live Termites

Seeing live termites, either the worker termites (pale, ant-like insects) or the winged swarmers, is the most definitive sign of an infestation. While worker termites are rarely seen in the open, swarmers are often visible during swarming season. If you see what appear to be ants with straight antennae and equal-sized wings, you are likely seeing termites. Distinguishing between ants and termites is crucial, as their treatments differ. Termites have straight antennae, uniform wing size, and a broad waist, unlike ants which have elbowed antennae, unequal wing size, and a narrow waist. If you spot these insects, it's important to contact a professional immediately for identification and treatment.

Ignoring any of these signs can lead to significant and costly damage to your property. Early detection and prompt action are essential for effective termite control.
